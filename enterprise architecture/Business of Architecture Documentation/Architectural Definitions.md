# Enterprise Architecture Glossary Overview
## Purpose
This document provides concise definitions and practical examples of key terms used in Enterprise Architecture for all employees.

---

### Technical Decision
**Definition:**
A choice made to address a specific technical need or problem within the organization’s IT environment.

**Example:**
Selecting a cloud provider (e.g., AWS vs. Azure) for hosting company applications.

---

### Architectural Standard
**Definition:**
A documented guideline specifying preferred technologies, patterns, or practices to ensure consistency across systems.

**Example:**
Mandating the use of RESTful APIs for all internal data integrations.

---

### Architectural Principles
**Definition:**
High-level rules or guidelines that shape and govern architectural decisions and align technology with business goals.

**Example:**
“Prioritize security by design in all application development.”

---

### Waivers
**Definition:**
Formal approvals allowing an exception to established architectural standards or principles.

**Example:**
Permitting a legacy system to use SOAP APIs instead of REST due to integration constraints.

---

### Governance
**Definition:**
Processes and structures for overseeing, guiding, and ensuring adherence to architectural standards and principles.

**Example:**
A review board evaluates major IT projects for compliance with EA guidelines.

---

### Compliance
**Definition:**
The act of adhering to established policies, standards, and regulations within the architecture framework.

**Example:**
All deployed applications must pass annual security audits as per company policy.

---

### Reference Architecture
**Definition:**
A template solution describing best practices and patterns for recurring design problems within a domain.

**Example:**
A standard blueprint for implementing microservices in the organization.

---

### Roadmap
**Definition:**
A strategic plan outlining the evolution and implementation of architectural initiatives over time.

**Example:**
A two-year plan to migrate all on-premises systems to the cloud.

---
